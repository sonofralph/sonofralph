## Executive Summary

Mise has a product problem most SaaS startups would kill for: it's already better than most of what exists at a third of the price. The problem is nobody knows it exists yet.

This playbook closes that gap. Not with five channels at once. With one dominant channel, one clear ICP, and a ruthless 90-day sequence designed to get to 10 paying customers before spending a single pound or dollar on ads.

**Current estimated success probability:** 30–40%
**Target after executing this playbook:** 70%+
**The delta is entirely distribution. Not product.**

## 1. Market Analysis

### The Landscape
The hospitality inventory management market is underserved at the SMB level. Here's who's in it:

| Competitor | Price | Problem |
|---|---|---|
| MarketMan | $200–400/mo | Too expensive for small ops, over-complex |
| Apicbase | $300+/mo | Enterprise-only, long implementation |
| Crunchtime | Enterprise | Too big, requires a consultant |
| Lightspeed / Toast (bundled) | POS-tied | Shallow inventory, vendor lock-in |
| Spreadsheets | Free | No structure, no audit trail, breaks at scale |

**The real competitor is not another SaaS. It's Excel.**

Over 60% of independent hospitality businesses manage inventory on spreadsheets or paper. They know it's broken. They've heard of software solutions. They haven't adopted because the solutions either cost too much or look too complicated for their team.

### The Opportunity
Mise lands at $49/mo — 4–8x cheaper than MarketMan or Apicbase. It has feature parity or better on the core workflows (stock movements, purchase orders, wastage, alerts, recipes). It onboards in under 20 minutes.

This is a genuine price/value disruption in a market that hasn't been disrupted at the SMB level.

## 2. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Do not try to sell to everyone. Pick one sub-vertical, own it, get 10 testimonials, then expand.

### Primary ICP — Phase 1 (0–6 months)

**Who:** Independent restaurant groups with 2–5 locations

**Why this specific profile:**
- Single-location restaurants don't feel multi-location pain acutely (they use one spreadsheet and it mostly works)
- 5+ locations often have existing systems or dedicated ops managers who are harder to move
- 2–5 locations is the sweet spot: they're feeling the chaos of coordinating across sites, they're spending real money on waste, but they haven't committed to enterprise software yet
- The owner or ops manager makes the buying decision — short sales cycle, no IT department to navigate

**Geography (start here, expand later):** UK and Ireland
- Strong restaurant density in London, Dublin, Manchester, Edinburgh
- Hospitality sector culturally predisposed to informal supplier relationships and referral-based software adoption
- VAT/tax structure is different from US, but Mise doesn't need to handle that at MVP — it's an IMS not an accounting tool
- If founder is based here, relationship-building is easier

**Firmographic profile:**
- 2–5 locations
- Independent (not a franchise)
- Annual revenue: £500K–£5M
- Staff: 10–80 people
- Uses WhatsApp groups for shift handovers (huge pain signal)
- Has had a "waste problem" conversation in the last 6 months
- Does stocktakes on paper or in a shared Google Sheet

**Pain triggers that signal readiness to buy:**
- New head chef or ops manager just joined and wants to "get organised"
- Just opened a second location and the first-location system is breaking down
- Recently had a stock discrepancy or theft incident
- Owner is spending Sundays manually reconciling spreadsheets

### Secondary ICP — Phase 2 (6–12 months)
Hotel F&B departments (food and beverage managers who are separate from the hotel PMS and have no dedicated inventory tool), then café groups, then catering companies.

## 4. Primary Channel Strategy

### The Rule: One Channel First

Most early-stage SaaS founders spread across five channels and master none. The playbook is: go deep on one channel, get to 10 customers, extract learnings, then expand.

### The Primary Channel: Direct Warm Outreach + Referral Network

**Why this first, not ads, not SEO:**
- Ads require budget you don't have and data you haven't collected yet
- SEO takes 6–18 months to produce organic leads
- Warm outreach gets you customers in 2–4 weeks
- The conversations tell you what messaging works before you spend money amplifying it

**The channel in practice:**
1. Map every person in the founder's network who works in hospitality or knows someone who does
2. Go two degrees out — "do you know anyone who runs a restaurant group?"
3. Offer a free 30-minute setup call and 3-month free trial for early adopters willing to give honest feedback
4. Convert feedback + usage into a testimonial and a case study
5. Ask every early customer: "who else do you know with the same problem?"

This is not glamorous. It is how every successful B2B SaaS company found its first 10 customers.

### Secondary Channel (Activate at Month 2): Hospitality Accountants

One accountant who does the books for 30 restaurants is worth 30 cold leads who've never heard of you.

Find 5–10 hospitality-specialist accountants and bookkeepers. Offer them:
- A referral commission (10–20% of first year's revenue per referral)
- A white-label or co-branded demo they can show clients
- Priority support for any client they bring in

Accountants are trusted advisors to their clients. When they recommend software, it converts at 3–5x the rate of a cold ad.

## 5. First 10 Customers Playbook

### The Philosophy
The goal of the first 10 customers is not revenue. It is:
- Proof that the product solves a real problem
- Testimonials for the website
- Case studies for content
- Referrals to the next 20 customers

Don't optimise for LTV yet. Optimise for learning.

### The Sequence

**Week 1: List Building**
- Build a list of 50 target prospects (2–5 location restaurant groups in target geography)
- Sources: LinkedIn, Google Maps searches ("restaurant group [city]"), local food media, Instagram accounts of multi-site operators
- For each: find the owner/ops manager name, LinkedIn, email if possible

**Week 2: First Outreach Wave**
- Send personalised outreach to the top 25 on the list
- Message format: short, specific, no pitch deck, ask for a 15-minute call

> *"Hi [Name], I saw that you're running [Restaurant Group] across [X] sites. I built a tool specifically for multi-location restaurant inventory — it replaces the spreadsheet chaos, takes 20 minutes to set up, and costs £49/mo. Would you be open to a 15-minute call? I'll show you exactly how it would work for your operation. No sales pitch."*

- Follow up once, 5 days later. If no response, move on.

**Week 3–4: Demo Calls**
- Demo structure: 20 minutes showing their specific workflow
  - "How do you currently track stock across your locations?" → show how Mise solves that exact scenario
  - Show the mobile experience (chefs logging quickly)
  - Show the dashboard they'd check in the morning
  - Don't demo every feature. Demo the 3 that solve their specific pain.
- Offer: "Try it free for 3 months. I'll set it up with you in a 30-minute session. If it's not useful by month 1, cancel — no hard feelings."

**Week 5–8: Onboarding Early Customers**
- Do white-glove onboarding for the first 10 — personally
- Seed their items, locations, categories from their existing spreadsheets
- Check in at day 7, day 14, day 30
- At day 30: ask for a testimonial and a referral

**Month 2: Second Outreach Wave**
- Use learnings from the first wave to refine messaging
- Reach out to the next 25 on the list, now with social proof from the first customers

### What "10 Customers" Unlocks

| Milestone | What It Enables |
|---|---|
| 3 customers | Enough to call yourself "trusted by hospitality operators" |
| 5 customers | First case study. First testimonial for the website. |
| 10 customers | Social proof to run ads. Enough data for SEO content. Referral flywheel starting. |
| 10 paying (Post-trial) | Unit economics data. Go/no-go on paid acquisition. |

## 6. Partnership Strategy

### Tier 1 — Highest Priority (Month 1–3)

**Hospitality Accountants & Bookkeepers**
- Targets: Firms that specifically advertise hospitality clients (search "hospitality accountant [city]")
- Approach: Email the partner, offer a 20-minute intro call, focus on how Mise makes their client's accounts easier to reconcile (clean audit logs, CSV exports)
- Deal: 15–20% recurring referral commission for lifetime of referred customer

**Restaurant Consultants**
- Who: People who set up new restaurants, help operators expand, write Standard Operating Procedures
- Approach: Same as accountants. They are trusted at the exact moment a new restaurant owner needs software.
- Deal: Referral commission + co-branded onboarding guide they can give clients

### Tier 2 — Medium Priority (Month 3–6)

**POS Vendors Without Deep Inventory**
- Square, Zettle, SumUp, EPOS Now, Lightspeed (at SMB tier)
- These systems have basic inventory but operators consistently outgrow them
- Approach: Reach out to their "technology partner" or "integrations" team. Position Mise as complementary, not competitive.
- Long term: Build a lightweight POS sync integration to deepen the relationship

**Food & Beverage Distributors**
- Local produce suppliers, foodservice wholesalers, specialist importers
- Their operators are your exact ICP
- Approach: Offer to mention them as a recommended supplier in Mise's onboarding. In return, they mention Mise in their newsletter or sales calls.

### Tier 3 — Long Term (Month 6+)

**Industry Associations**
- UK Hospitality, British Beer & Pub Association, Restaurant Association of Ireland, etc.
- Slow to move but: member databases of hundreds or thousands of operators
- Approach: Sponsor a webinar, speak at a regional event, get listed in their "preferred supplier" directory
- These relationships build credibility more than they drive immediate volume

## 8. Pricing Strategy Analysis

### Current Pricing
- Free: 1 location, 3 users, 50 items — permanent
- Pro: £/\$49/mo — 5 locations, 20 users, unlimited items, 14-day trial
- Enterprise: Custom

### What's Working
- The 14-day trial with no card required removes the biggest conversion barrier in B2B SaaS
- The Free tier gives genuine value and creates a natural upgrade path
- £49 is genuinely disruptive vs. competitors

### What to Watch
- **Annual pricing:** Offer 2 months free on annual (£490/yr vs £588/yr). Improves cash flow and reduces churn. Add this at Month 3 once you have some monthly customers to convert.
- **Free tier limits:** 50 items is tight for a restaurant that might have 80–100 SKUs. Consider raising to 100 items once you have paid traction — it makes the free tier more genuinely useful as a lead generation tool without materially impacting conversion.
- **Enterprise pricing:** Don't publish a price. "Talk to us" is correct. Enterprise deals should be minimum £500/mo to be worth the customisation and support overhead.

### Pricing Page Conversion Optimisation
Once live at /pricing (done ✅), the next test is:
1. Add logos of customer types (restaurant icon, hotel icon, café icon) above the fold — "built for businesses like yours"
2. Add one testimonial on the Pro card specifically
3. Test "Most Popular" vs "Best Value" as the Pro badge
4. Add a "What's included in every plan" anchor link in the hero

### Day 1–14: Foundation
- [ ] Pricing page live (done ✅)

### Day 15–30: First Wave
- [ ] Send outreach to 25 prospects
- [ ] Book minimum 5 demo calls
- [ ] Begin writing "Complete Guide to Restaurant Stocktakes" (priority SEO piece)
- [ ] Identify 3 hospitality accountants to approach for partnerships
- [ ] Follow up with all non-responders (once, on day 5 after initial)

### Day 31–60: Close and Learn
- [ ] Convert minimum 3 from demo calls to free trial
- [ ] White-glove onboard each trial user (30-min setup call)
- [ ] Publish stocktake guide + release free template
- [ ] Begin email outreach to hospitality accountants
- [ ] Second outreach wave to remaining 25 prospects
- [ ] At day 45: check in with all trial users — any friction? any feedback?

### Day 61–90: Social Proof + Referrals
- [ ] Request testimonials from active trial users
- [ ] Write first case study (with permission from a trial customer)
- [ ] Add testimonials and case study to /pricing page
- [ ] Ask each active trial user for a referral: "who else do you know with the same problem?"
- [ ] Publish second content piece (par level guide)
- [ ] Begin accountant partnership conversations in earnest
- [ ] Evaluate: are trial users converting to paid? If not — why? (talk to them directly)

### Month 3: Paid Acquisition Test
Only start paid ads once:
- You have 5 testimonials on the website
- You have at least one case study
- Your onboarding converts >60% of trials to 30-day active users
- You know your best-performing message from outreach experiments

Then: test £500–1,000/mo on Google Search for 1–2 high-intent terms ("restaurant inventory software UK"). Measure cost per trial, cost per paid customer. Scale only if unit economics work.

## 12. Competitive Differentiation Summary

**Mise wins on:**
1. **Price** — 4–8x cheaper than the next comparable solution
2. **Speed to value** — 20-minute onboarding vs days or weeks for enterprise tools
3. **Hospitality-specificity** — shift handovers, requisitions, departments, expiry tracking built in
4. **Full audit trail** — often required for compliance, rarely available at this price point
5. **No vendor lock-in** — data export always available (now including GDPR export)

**Mise needs to build:**
1. **Social proof** — first 10 testimonials are the priority
2. **Awareness** — nobody knows it exists yet
3. **Trust** — new SaaS products face a "will this company still exist next year?" concern. Counter with: visible founder, responsive support, transparent roadmap.

## 13. Founder Presence Strategy

B2B SaaS at this stage sells on trust. Trust comes from knowing who built the product and why.

**Recommended:**
- Founder posts weekly on LinkedIn about hospitality operations, waste reduction, inventory management — not about Mise directly, but about the problems Mise solves
- After each demo call, connect with the prospect on LinkedIn
- Share customer wins publicly (with permission): "One of our restaurant partners reduced their weekly waste review from 3 hours to 20 minutes"
- Be visible in 1–2 hospitality Facebook groups or communities — answer questions about inventory, don't pitch

This compounds. A hospitality operator who has seen the founder's name 10 times before getting a cold outreach will respond at 3x the rate of a cold stranger.
